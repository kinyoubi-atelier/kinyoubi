- In a professional and workmanlike manner

## 4. Fees and Payment

### 4.1 Fee Structure

Fees will be specified in each SOW and will be one of the following:
- **Fixed Fee:** A set amount for the entire engagement
- **Time & Materials (T&M):** Billed based on hours worked at an agreed hourly rate, plus documented out-of-pocket expenses
- **Hybrid:** A retainer (fixed monthly fee) plus T&M overage, or other hybrid structure as specified in the SOW

### 4.2 Currency and Payment Terms

**Currency:**  
Fees may be invoiced in:
- USD (United States Dollars)
- EUR (Euros)
- GBP (British Pounds)
- INR (Indian Rupees)

Currency will be specified in the SOW. All amounts are exclusive of applicable taxes.

**Payment Terms:**  
- **Default:** Net 30 days from invoice date (payment due within 30 days)
- **SOW-Specific:** The SOW may specify different terms (e.g., Net 15, Net 60, or deposit + milestone payments)
- **Payment Method:** Wire transfer, ACH, credit card, or other method specified by Kinyoubi

### 4.3 Late Payment

If payment is not received within 30 days of the invoice due date:
- Kinyoubi may charge late interest at the rate of **1.5% per month** (or the maximum rate permitted by law, whichever is lower)
- Kinyoubi may suspend work on the engagement until payment is current
- Kinyoubi may terminate the engagement if payment is more than 60 days overdue

### 4.4 Invoicing

Kinyoubi will issue invoices:
- For fixed-fee SOWs: upon completion of milestones or at intervals specified in the SOW
- For T&M SOWs: monthly in arrears, with a detailed breakdown of hours worked and expenses incurred
- For retainer + T&M: the retainer is due on the first of each month; overage T&M is billed the following month

All invoices must be paid in accordance with Section 4.2 above.

### 4.5 Out-of-Pocket Expenses

For T&M and hybrid engagements, Kinyoubi may bill documented out-of-pocket expenses, including:
- Travel (flights, accommodation, ground transport) at cost plus [TBD — 10%] handling
- Software licenses, third-party services, and tools required for the SOW
- Testing and hosting infrastructure

Expenses require prior written approval from the Client unless the SOW specifies a budget cap.

### 4.6 Taxes

All fees are exclusive of applicable sales tax, VAT, GST, or other taxes. The Client is responsible for:
- Any applicable taxes on services rendered in the Client's jurisdiction
- Providing tax identification numbers and withholding documentation as required by law
- Remitting taxes to appropriate authorities

Kinyoubi will provide tax invoices or receipts as required by law.

### 4.7 No Refunds

Except where required by law or as specified in the SOW, fees are non-refundable once work has commenced.

## 5. Intellectual Property

### 5.1 Work Product Assignment

**On receipt of full payment,** all intellectual property rights in the work product created specifically for the Client under an SOW ("Work Product") are assigned exclusively to the Client, including:
- Custom code, scripts, and software developed for the Client
- Documentation, diagrams, and technical specifications created for the Client
- Data, databases, or configurations created specifically for the Client
- Derivative works and modifications of third-party code (to the extent assignable)

**Condition:** Assignment is contingent on full payment. If payment is not made, Kinyoubi retains all rights and may use the Work Product to offset fees.

### 5.2 Background Intellectual Property

Kinyoubi retains all rights to:
- Pre-existing tools, frameworks, libraries, and methodologies developed prior to the engagement (collectively, "Background IP")
- General skills, experience, and knowledge acquired during the engagement
- Concepts, ideas, and processes developed independently by Kinyoubi
- Any improvements or modifications to Kinyoubi's Background IP

The Client receives a non-exclusive, royalty-free license to use Background IP incorporated into the Work Product, limited to the Client's internal business purposes.

### 5.3 Third-Party Code and Open-Source

If the Work Product incorporates open-source code:
- The SOW will disclose the open-source licenses (e.g., MIT, Apache 2.0, GPL)
- The Client agrees to comply with all applicable open-source license terms
- The assignment in Section 5.1 does not affect third parties' rights under open-source licenses

If the Work Product incorporates third-party commercial code:
- The SOW will identify the third-party code and licensing terms
- The Client is responsible for obtaining necessary third-party licenses and permissions

### 5.4 Client Materials

The Client retains all intellectual property rights in materials, data, or content provided to Kinyoubi ("Client Materials"). Kinyoubi receives a license to use Client Materials only as necessary to provide the services described in the SOW.

### 5.5 Intellectual Property Indemnity

Kinyoubi indemnifies the Client against third-party claims that the Work Product (excluding Client Materials and third-party code) infringes a third party's intellectual property rights, provided:
- The Client notifies Kinyoubi in writing within 30 days of learning of the claim
- Kinyoubi has sole control of the defense and settlement
- The claim does not arise from the Client's modification of the Work Product without Kinyoubi's consent

Kinyoubi's remedy is to either:
- Obtain the right for the Client to continue using the Work Product
- Modify the Work Product to make it non-infringing
- If neither is commercially reasonable, terminate the SOW and refund fees paid for that SOW

## 6. Confidentiality

### 6.1 Definition

"Confidential Information" means any information disclosed by one party to the other that is marked as confidential or would reasonably be understood as confidential, including:
- Business plans, strategies, and financial information
- Software code, algorithms, and technical documentation
- Client data, customer lists, and personal information
- Security procedures and encryption keys
- Pricing and fee information

### 6.2 Permitted Use

Each party may use Confidential Information only for the purposes of performing obligations or exercising rights under this MSA. Kinyoubi may not disclose Confidential Information to third parties except:
- To sub-processors or contractors who need access and who are bound by equivalent confidentiality obligations
- As required by law or court order (with notice to the other party where legally permitted)
- To supervisory authorities or regulators investigating a data protection complaint

### 6.3 Exclusions

Confidential Information does not include information that:
- Is publicly available (not through breach of confidence)
- Was known prior to disclosure (with written evidence)
- Is independently developed by the receiving party (with written evidence)
- Is rightfully received from a third party without confidentiality restrictions

### 6.4 Term

Confidentiality obligations survive termination of this MSA for **3 years** from the date of disclosure, or for the period required by law (whichever is longer).

### 6.5 Return or Destruction

Upon termination, each party will return or securely destroy all Confidential Information in the other party's possession, except as required by law to retain (and confidentiality will continue to apply).

## 8. Limitation of Liability

### 8.1 Liability Cap

**Except as provided in Section 8.2 below, neither party's total liability under this MSA for any 12-month period will exceed the total fees paid (or payable) by the Client to Kinyoubi in that 12-month period.**

If the Client has paid no fees (e.g., due to a pricing dispute), the liability cap is USD $1,000 (or equivalent).

### 8.2 Exceptions to the Cap

The liability cap does not apply to:
1. **Confidentiality breaches:** Disclosure of Confidential Information
2. **Intellectual property indemnity:** Claims covered by Section 5.5
3. **Data protection indemnity:** Breaches of the Data Processing Addendum (Section 9)
4. **Gross negligence or willful misconduct:** Actions taken with reckless disregard or intentional wrongdoing
5. **Either party's indemnification obligations** under this MSA
6. **Death or personal injury** caused by negligence

### 8.3 Indirect Damages

**Neither party will be liable for:**
- Lost profits, revenue, or business opportunity
- Lost or corrupted data
- Loss of use or interruption of business
- Indirect, incidental, special, or consequential damages

even if advised of the possibility of such damages.

### 8.4 Mitigation

Each party is responsible for taking reasonable steps to mitigate damages. Failure to mitigate reduces the other party's liability.

## 10. Termination

### 10.1 Termination for Convenience

Either party may terminate an SOW (and the services under that SOW only) by providing **30 days' written notice** to the other party.

**Effect:**
- Kinyoubi will cease performing services at the end of the 30-day notice period
- The Client must pay for all services performed and expenses incurred through the termination date
- Kinyoubi will deliver or return all work product and Client Materials within 30 days of termination
- Deliverables completed as of the termination date will be delivered; incomplete deliverables may be withheld until payment is current

### 10.2 Termination for Cause

Either party may terminate an SOW immediately (without notice) if:
- The other party materially breaches this MSA or the SOW and does not cure the breach within **15 business days** of written notice
- The other party becomes insolvent, bankrupt, or subject to receivership
- The other party's breach threatens the confidentiality, security, or integrity of personal data or systems

### 10.3 Effect of Termination

Upon termination:
- Kinyoubi's obligation to perform services ends
- The Client's obligation to pay for services rendered continues
- All sections of this MSA that by their nature survive termination (Confidentiality, Intellectual Property, Liability, Governing Law) remain in effect
- Data protection obligations survive until all personal data is returned or deleted per the DPA

### 10.4 Survival

The following sections survive termination indefinitely:
- Section 5 (Intellectual Property)
- Section 6 (Confidentiality)
- Section 8 (Limitation of Liability)
- Section 9 (Data Protection)
- Section 11 (Indemnification)
- Section 12 (Governing Law and Dispute Resolution)

## 19. Force Majeure

### 19.1 Definition

"Force Majeure Event" means an event beyond either party's reasonable control, including:
- Natural disasters (earthquake, flood, hurricane, extreme weather)
- War, terrorism, or civil unrest
- Pandemic or epidemic (including COVID-19)
- Government action or sanction
- Telecommunications or internet outage
- Utility failure (power, water, internet)

**Excluded:** Events caused by a party's negligence, breach, or failure to prepare or maintain adequate contingencies.

### 19.2 Effect

If a Force Majeure Event prevents a party from performing obligations:
- That party is excused from performance for the duration of the event (plus a reasonable recovery period)
- The excused party must provide written notice within 5 business days of the event
- The non-performing party remains liable for fees accrued prior to the event (unless the event prevents payment receipt)

### 19.3 Ongoing Obligation

The parties will work together to resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable. If a Force Majeure Event prevents performance for more than 60 days, either party may terminate the affected SOW without penalty.
